A Culturally Aproppriated Oriental Dish!

You want a cheap meal with vague asian flare? You have come to the right place my destitute friend. For roughly $15 (store dependant), I will show you how to create this easy and tasty dish!

Ingredients

  • 1 box cheapest linguine or spaghetti noodles in pasta aisle
  • 1 bottle your choice of stir fry sauce
  • Pack of pre-cut stirfry beef or stew chunked beef
  • 1 bag frozen vegetable medley of choice
  • (Optional) Sesame seeds
  • (Optional) 1 Bottle soy sauce or 3 packets from leftover chinese takeout

Steps

Optional Prep

Put beef in a ziplock bag and cover with soy sauce. Store in fridge as long as you want marinaded before cooking.

Cooking

  1. Get out a large pot and fill with water. Throw that on the stove top and crank the burner up to max. once the water starts boiling turn the heat down to medium and dump the whole box of noodles in there. Make sure to stir occasionally while doing the other steps.
  2. Grab a big pan and pour a bit of veg oil in there. Open vegetable medley bag and put desired quantity of vegetables in the pan. Turn the heat up to an 8, those veggies are frozen! Once everything starts to sizzle start stirring and mixing everything around slowly to ensure veggies are cooked evenly.
  3. Once the veggies are almost cooked turn the heat down to 6 and add in the beef. It will fry much quicker than the veggies. Continue to stir slowly to ensure even cooking.
  4. Check the noodles as they should be done by now. If noodles are at suffiecient level of malleability, strain water out and turn off extra burner.
  5. Once beef and veggies are done, add all the noodles into the pan and pour an adequate helping of sauce over the whole thing. Start rapidly stirring until all contents of pan are evenly coated. Take pan off heat.
  6. Serve immediate helpings and store the massive quantity of leftovers.
  7. (Optional) Sprinkle sesame seeds on top of servings to make you look fancy to your friends.
